Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  1. Item Name
    • This is the name of the product. Example: "Fortune Oil 1L" or "Red Notebook".
    • It helps you and others easily identify the item.
  2. SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)
    • Think of this as the unique ID code for the item.
    • Each product should have its own SKU, so the system knows exactly which item it is.
    • Example: "OIL-1L-F1001" or "NOTE-A5-BLUE01".
  3. Quantity
    • This tells how many pieces or units of the item are currently available.
    • Example: "50" bottles of oil or "200" notebooks.
  4. Unit
    • This shows the measurement type of the item.
    • Example: pieces, liters, kilograms, boxes.
    • So if you write 10 and unit is kg, it means you have 10 kilograms of that item.
  5. Category
    • The group or type of item. Categories make searching easier.
    • Example: Food, Stationery, Electronics, Clothes.
  6. Selling Price
    • The price at which you are selling the item to your customers.
    • Example: If you bought/purchase the notebook for ₹18.99 and you want to sell the notebook for ₹29.99, then selling price is 29.99.
  7. Purchase Price
    • The price at which you bought the item from the supplier.
    • Example: If you bought the notebook for ₹24.99, then purchase price is 24.99 .
  8. Supplier Name
    • The name of the supplier/vendor from whom you bought the item.
    • Example: "ABC Stationery Supplier" or "XYZ Wholesale Market".
  9. Date Added
    • The date when you added this item into your inventory system.
    • This helps track how old the stock is.
  10. Upload Image
    • You can upload a photo of the item.
    • This helps in quickly identifying the product by looking at its picture.
  11. Description
    • A short note about the item. You can write extra details that don’t fit in other fields.
    • Example: "Blue cover, A5 size, 200 pages" or "Fortune Sunflower Oil, refined, 1-liter bottle".

  1. Bottle (btl)
    • For liquids or drinks that come in bottles.
    • Example: cold drink bottle, oil bottle, sauce bottle.
  2. Box (box)
    • A larger packaging unit containing many items inside.
    • Example: a box of tea bags, a box of chocolates, a carton of soaps.
  3. Bundle (bdl)
    • A tied pack of multiple items.
    • Example: a bundle of currency notes, a bundle of broomsticks, a bundle of notebooks.
  4. Dozen (doz)
    • A set of 12 pieces.
    • Example: 1 dozen eggs = 12 eggs, 1 dozen bananas = 12 bananas.
  5. Gram (g)
    • Smallest weight unit for light items.
    • Example: 100 g turmeric powder, 50 g tea leaves.
  6. Jar (jar)
    • For items sold in jars.
    • Example: pickle jar, jam jar, honey jar.
  7. Kilogram (kg)
    • Standard unit for heavier items.
    • Example: 1 kg sugar, 2 kg rice, 5 kg wheat flour.
  8. Liter (L)
    • Standard unit for large liquid quantities.
    • Example: 1 liter milk, 2 liter soft drink, 5 liter water can.
  9. Milligram (mg)
    • 1 mg = 0.001 g.
    • Example: 250 mg tablet (for medical/general items)
  10. Milliliter (ml)
    • For small liquid quantities.
    • Example: 200 ml cold drink, 500 ml oil pouch.
  11. Packet (pkt)
    • Used for items packed in small sealed packs.
    • Example: biscuit packet, chips packet, spice packet.
  12. Pair (pr)
    • For items sold in twos.
    • Example: a pair of slippers, a pair of socks.
  13. Pieces (pcs)
    • Used for single items that are sold one by one.
    • Example: 1 soap bar, 1 notebook, 1 packet of chips.
  14. Quintal (qtl)
    • 1 quintal = 100 kg.
    • Example: 1 quintal rice sack.
  15. Roll
    • Used for items that are wound up or packed in a cylindrical shape.
    • Example: 1 roll of tissue paper, 1 roll of aluminum foil, 1 roll of butter paper.
  16. Sachet
    • A small sealed packet containing a single_use or small quantity of a product.
    • Example: 1 sachet of shampoo, 1 sachet of ketchup, 1 sachet of coffee.
  17. Set
    • A collection of items that are sold together as a single group.
    • Example: 1 set of cups and saucers, 1 set of spoons, 1 set of plates.
  18. Tin (tin)
    • Used for items packed in tins.
    • Example: ghee tin, powdered milk tin, paint tin.
  19. Unit
    • A general term when the item is sold individually without a specific measurement.
    • Example: 1 unit of soap, 1 unit of notebook, 1 unit of detergent bottle.

  1. Bakery
    • Freshly baked or packaged bread items and sweets.
    • Examples: bread, buns, biscuits, cookies, cakes, pastries.
  2. Beverages
    • All types of drinks, ready-to-drink or for making at home.
    • Examples: soft drinks, bottled water, fruit juices, tea, coffee, energy drinks.
  3. Cleaning Supplies
    • Products used for cleaning and laundry.
    • Examples: detergent, dishwashing liquid, floor cleaner, broom, mop, sponges.
  4. Dairy & Eggs
    • Milk-based and fresh dairy products, plus eggs.
    • Examples: milk, curd (yogurt), butter, ghee, cheese, paneer, eggs.
  5. Frozen Foods
    • Pre-packed food stored in freezers.
    • Examples: frozen peas, French fries, ice cream, frozen nuggets, parathas.
  6. Fruits
    • Fresh fruits sold by weight or pieces.
    • Examples: apples, bananas, mangoes, grapes, oranges.
  7. Grocery & Food Items
    • Basic food essentials used daily.
    • Examples: rice, wheat flour (atta), pulses (dal), sugar, salt, cooking oil, spices.
  8. Healthcare
    • Basic over-the-counter medical and hygiene items.
    • Examples: bandages, antiseptics, hand sanitizer, first-aid supplies, masks, basic medicines (if allowed).
  9. Household Essentials
    • Everyday non-food items for the home.
    • Examples: matchboxes, candles, batteries, utensils, plastic containers, aluminum foil.
  10. Miscellaneous
    • For items that don’t fit into other categories.
    • Examples: seasonal products, gifts, unique store-specific goods.
  11. Personal Care
    • Items for hygiene and grooming.
    • Examples: soap, shampoo, conditioner, toothpaste, toothbrush, shaving cream, hair oil.
  12. Snacks
    • Ready-to-eat items for quick consumption.
    • Examples: chips, wafers, chocolates, namkeen, instant noodles.
  13. Stationery
    • Paper and office/school-related items.
    • Examples: pens, pencils, erasers, notebooks, glue, markers.
  14. Toys & Kids Items
    • Small toys and products mainly for children.
    • Examples: balls, dolls, coloring books, crayons, puzzles.
  15. Vegetables
    • Fresh vegetables sold by weight.
    • Examples: potatoes, onions, tomatoes, spinach, capsicum, cauliflower.

  1. Purchase Order ID
    • A unique identifier for each purchase order.
    • Helps track and manage orders efficiently.
    • Example: "PO001", "PO002".
  2. Store Owner Contact Number
    • A shop owner or shop phone number you can write here.
    • It help supplier to contact you if something goes wrong with delivery.
  3. Expected Delivery Date
    • The date when you expect the ordered items to arrive.
  4. Supplier Name
    • The name of the supplier for the order items.
    • Suppliers name that have any item which a quantity is 5 or less are shown in the selection list automatically.
    • Example: if the blue pen supplied by "Rorito PVT LTD" has a quantity of 5 or less then "Rorito PVT LTD" will appear in the list.
  5. Item Name
    • The name of the item being reordered. Items which has a quantity of 5 or less are listed based on supplier name for selection.
    • Example: If you select "Rorito PVT LTD" which has a items with quantity of 5 or less, such as "Blue Pen", "Notebook", or "Cooking Oil 1L", are listed in the selection.
  6. Quantity
    • The number of items you want to reorder.
    • Example: if you want to order 3000 Ball pen packet then quantity will be 3000.
  7. Delivery Address
    • Address where you want to receive the items
    • Example: "123 Main St/Area, Nearby, City, State, PIN Code".
  8. Remarks
    • A note for the supplier to read.
    • Example: The supplied items should arrive before 6:00 pm, as after that my store will be closed for the day.

  1. Customer ID
    • A unique identifier for each customer.
    • Helps track purchases and manage customer accounts.
    • Example: "CUST001", "CUST002".
  2. Customer Name
    • A name of the customer who buying.
    • Example: "Ram" or "Krishna".
  3. Customer Phone Number
    • A customer contact number for marketing.
  4. Sales Date
    • A date when items is sold.
    • This helps track daily sales.
  5. Payment Method
    • A payment mode: cash or online, which tells us how customer was pay for items.
  6. Item Name
    • Name of item that customer is buying. A items with quantity is 5 or less will not be shows in a list.
    • Example: "Fortune Oil 1L" or "Red Notebook" and Blue pen with quantity 3 is not showing in the list.
  7. Quantity
    • A number of item that customer is buying.
    • Example: If customer buying a 6 notebook then quantity will be 6.
  8. Price
    • A price of item is selling price that you entered at the time of adding items in the inventory.
    • It gets automatically entered by the system when you select an item.
    • Example: if Blue pen selling price is 7 then price will be 7.
  9. Discount Mode
    • A discount mode: cash or percentage %, which tells how much you offer discount and price get adjusted automatically.
  10. Amount
    • A total amount of per item that customer is buying.
    • It gets automatically calculated by the system when you select an item and enter quantity and discount mode.
    • Example: If customer buying a 6 notebook then amount will be 42 after discount.
  11. Total Amount
    • A total amount of all items that customer is buying
    • Example: If customer buying a 6 notebook with selling price 70 and 3 pen with selling price 7 then total amount will be 441 after no discount.
